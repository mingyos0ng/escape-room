INCIDENT REPORT — AUTOMATED DISTRESS LOG
STARDATE 2347.062 — 94 DAYS AGO

You are Engineer Mira Kovač, a systems specialist aboard the deep-space research vessel Callisto IV. En route to retrieve a missing science crew, your ship sustained critical damage from an uncharted debris field and was forced to dock with the nearest structure: Erebus Station, a Helix Corp research outpost last reported operational 94 days ago.

Upon boarding, you find the station abandoned. No crew. No transmissions. The few logs that remain are corrupted or classified. Whatever happened here happened fast — half-eaten meals still sit in the mess hall; a chess game mid-move.

Your ship is gone. A catastrophic fuel leak ignited during docking and Callisto IV was destroyed. You are alone. Your only chance of rescue is to broadcast a distress signal from Erebus Station's communications array — but to do that, you'll need to restore power, oxygen, and the station's core systems first.

Emergency reserves give you 20 minutes of power. After that, life support fails permanently. Four critical modules must be restored in any order; each releases a security fragment required to unlock the escape pod. Move fast. Move smart.

    Restore all four station modules
    O2 Control, Reactor Bay, Communications Array, and Med Bay must each be repaired. Modules can be completed in any order — each releases one encrypted keycode fragment.
    PRIMARY
  • 02
    Collect all four keycode fragments
    Each repaired module drops one fragment (A–D). All four are required to unlock the escape pod's launch sequence. Fragments are saved automatically — you won't lose them if you navigate away.

    You have exactly 20 minutes of emergency power. The timer runs continuously across all rooms. If it reaches 00:00, life support fails and the mission is over.
